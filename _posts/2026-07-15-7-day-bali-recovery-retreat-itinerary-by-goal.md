# **7-Day Bali Recovery Retreat Itinerary by Goal**

A 7-day wellness retreat in Bali can reset much more than your out-of-office reply. With the right structure, one focused week can support your nervous system, sleep, digestion, and stress response in a way that a long weekend or casual holiday simply cannot. Less planning, fewer choices and expert-led days give your body space to switch off from constant decision-making and pressure.

Here we will walk through a simple, day-by-day shape for a 7-day stay. We will focus on four common goals: recovering from burnout, resetting sleep, calming digestion and building stress resilience. You will see what to schedule in the first 48 hours compared with days 5 to 7, and how this can shift slightly for solo guests, couples and small private groups.

## **Reset Your System, Not Just Your Out-of-Office**

A standard Bali escape often means late nights, rich food, cocktails and a lot of choices. That can be fun, but it rarely gives a deep reset. A structured, Ayurvedic-led week feels different. Your days are planned with you, treatments are sequenced and you are supported to rest, not perform.

On retreat, you can expect:

* Fewer decisions, clear routines, gentle timing
* Therapies matched to your body type and current state
* A calm arc from arrival shock to deeper rest and then to integration

Our four primary recovery goals often overlap, but each has a slightly different flavour. Burnout recovery can show up as deep fatigue, emotional flatness, and a “wired but tired” feeling. A sleep reset typically targets trouble getting to sleep, waking in the night, or jet lag. Digestive support often focuses on bloating, irregular bowels, and heaviness after meals. Stress resilience suits people whose lives will stay busy, but who need better tools to handle pressure without burning out.

The key is not to treat all seven days the same. What you schedule on days 1 and 2 should be very different from what you do on days 5 to 7.

## **Why the First 48 Hours Shape Your Entire Retreat**

The first two days are your landing window. You arrive from long-haul or interstate travel and may be dealing with:

* Dehydration and stiffness from sitting
* Extra caffeine and blue light from travel and work
* An emotional drop as you finally stop

Trying to squeeze in big detoxes or heavy activities in this window often backfires. The Ayurvedic way is assessment before intensity, so you begin with a detailed consultation and health history, pulse and tongue reading to understand your dosha balance, and gentle, grounding treatments instead of strong cleanses.

By goal, the ideal focus looks like this in the first 48 hours.

For burnout and stress resilience, the priority is a quiet arrival with a very simple schedule, clear tech boundaries set from day one, and soothing bodywork and breath rather than strong exercise.

For a sleep reset, you’ll focus on early morning light exposure to set the body clock, avoiding daytime naps even if you feel wiped, and keeping the first night very simple with an early lights-out routine.

For digestion, the emphasis is on warm, simple meals that are easy to digest, plenty of warm water and herbal infusions (without shock cleanses), and only gentle abdominal work if your system feels ready.

## **Day 1, 2: Grounding, Assessment and Gentle Reset**

A good arrival day is calm and uncluttered. For most guests, we suggest:

* Airport transfer and a soft welcome
* A light Balinese and Ayurvedic-inspired meal, not a feast
* A short orientation, then time alone to unpack and breathe
* An early night with no pressure to “make the most of it”

Privacy and discretion matter for founders, executives and public figures, so arrival is quiet, with minimal group contact if you prefer.

Day 2 is usually your first full “core” day and follows a gentle structure. In the morning, you’ll do gentle yoga or breathwork to wake the body, followed by an Ayurvedic consultation where your program is designed. Midday typically includes your first tailored treatment, often a warm oil massage for grounding, a first taste of Shirodhara for mental rest, or a very gentle digestive therapy. The afternoon is kept low-demand, such as pool time or garden rest, with guided rest rather than a free-for-all activity menu. In the evening, you’ll have an early, sleep-friendly dinner, then a simple meditation or Yoga Nidra, and a digital sunset to help your circadian rhythm reset.

Variations for different guests:

* **Men-focused:** we often focus on performance recovery, such as jet lag, training load and work stress, using grounding treatments instead of heavy detox or strict fasting at the start
* **Women-focused:** we take into account hormonal needs, cycle stage, fertility wishes or perimenopause, and allow more emotional decompression and quiet space
* **Small groups and couples:** we balance one shared element, such as evening meditation, with protected solo time so each person can follow their own treatment track from day one

## **Day 3, 4: Deepening Into Detox, Sleep Repair and Calm**

Once the body has landed and the nervous system has softened, the middle window of a 7-day wellness retreat in Bali is the safest time to deepen. You are more present, and your digestion, sleep and mood are usually more stable. This is often when treatments can become more targeted and when habits start to feel natural rather than forced.

By goal, intensity may shift like this:

* **Burnout:** more restorative bodywork (including longer oil treatments), nervous-system-focused breathwork or restorative yoga, and for some, gentle Panchakarma-style elements may begin if suitable
* **Sleep reset:** consistent wake-up time with morning light every day, gentle movement early and calming practices late, and evening treatments such as Shirodhara, head and foot massages and tailored herbal support
* **Digestion:** a personalised detox such as a simple kitchari or mono-diet, light fasting windows if appropriate for your body type, and digestive massages plus gut-calming yoga focused on twists and breath
* **Stress resilience:** more mental clarity work such as meditation coaching, pranayama for daily stress (not just retreat calm), and simple routines you can continue once you are home

Different guests will hold this stage differently:

* High-achieving Australians and cross-border operators may need short, pre-planned laptop check-ins that do not undo all the nervous system work
* Deep-healing international guests often value clear medical-style oversight and the option to slow the plan if detox feels too intense
* Couples and groups can plan one anchor ritual together each day, such as a shared treatment, a sunset ritual or evening reflection

## **Day 5, 7: Integration, Confidence and Long-Term Results**

The final days are not about pushing harder. By now your body has opened and released, so the focus moves to integration. This is how results last after you fly home.

A simple daily arc can look like:

* **Morning:** a little more energising movement for burnout and stress resilience, such as stronger yoga or nature walks, while those on deeper detox stick with softer options
* **Midday:** targeted treatments based on how you have responded so far, lighter if your system needs rest, deeper if you are strong and clear
* **Evening:** reflection, such as journaling or guided intention-setting, plus one final, balancing treatment and practical coaching for home

Tailoring for each goal is where the retreat becomes most practical. For burnout and high performers, the work often becomes very real-world: you’ll plan a realistic first 14 days back at work, set boundaries for meetings, travel and “catch-up,” and create a simple caffeine and alcohol plan while protecting your sleep window. For a sleep reset, you’ll map a home bedtime ritual you can keep, plan your light, screen and device use in the evening, and discuss travel routines including red-eye flight strategies. For digestion, you’ll leave with meal templates that work with your lifestyle, a clear pantry reset and what to avoid for the first 1 to 3 weeks, and agreement on daily basics like warm water, meal timing and chewing. For stress resilience, the emphasis is on making it doable: you’ll build a short “stress playbook” of practices under 15 minutes and choose a morning and evening anchor ritual you can keep even on busy days.

## **Sample 7-Day Retreat Shapes by Recovery Goal**

Here is how a 7-day stay might feel, without going minute by minute.

### **Burnout Recovery Week**

* **Days 1, 2:** grounding arrival, long sleep windows, gentle bodywork
* **Days 3, 4:** deeper restorative treatments, breathwork, low-stimulation afternoons
* **Days 5, 7:** slightly stronger movement, mindset work, work re-entry planning

### **Sleep Reset Week**

* **Days 1, 2:** strict light exposure routines, no naps, early nights
* **Days 3, 4:** consistent wake times, evening Shirodhara or head massage, herbal support
* **Days 5, 7:** refining your ideal sleep and wake times and creating a travel-friendly routine

### **Digestive Reset Week**

* **Days 1, 2:** soothing meals, warm fluids, gentle assessment
* **Days 3, 4:** deeper cleansing such as mono-diet, digestive treatments, calm yoga
* **Days 5, 7:** rebuilding strength, slightly wider food range, home meal plans

### **Stress Resilience Week**

* **Days 1, 2:** nervous system down-shifting, tech boundaries, basic breathwork
* **Days 3, 4:** meditation coaching, stress education, lifestyle tweaks
* **Days 5, 7:** creating and testing your simple daily routine for when life gets busy again

Persona notes are helpful for fine-tuning the same structure without changing its intent. Men-focused guests might take a break from intense training but keep light strength or mobility work, paired with enough nourishing food to protect muscle and energy while detoxing. Women-focused guests may shift timing of deeper therapies around their cycle and can focus on hormonal balance, fertility intentions or perimenopause support where appropriate. Small groups and couples often do best when shared experiences like sunset rituals, private dinners or group meditations are placed later in the day so they do not compete with core healing work earlier on.
